Types of Soils and Suitable Crops

1. Clay Soil

Characteristics:

Heavy, retains water well, and rich in nutrients but can be compacted easily.

Crops:

Rice, broccoli, cabbage, brussels sprouts, pumpkins.

2. Sandy Soli

Characteristics:

Light, well-draining, warms up quickly but doesn't retain nutrients well.

Crops:

Carrots, potatoes, radishes, peanuts, watermelon.

3. Loamy Soil

Characteristics:

Balanced mix of sand, silt, and clay, fertile, and has good drainage and moisture retention.

Crops:

Most garden vegetables (tomatoes, lettuce, peas, etc.), fruit trees, berries, corn.

4. Silty Soil

Characteristics:

Smooth, retains moisture well, and is fertile but can be poorly drained.

Crops:

Tomatoes, leafy greens (spinach, lettuce), strawberries, melons.

5. Peaty Soil

Characteristics:

High in organic matter, retains water well, acidic.

Crops:

Root vegetables (carrots, potatoes), legumes, and fruits like cranberries.

6. Chalky Soil

Characteristics:

Alkaline, stony, and free-draining but can be low in nutrients.

Crops:

Cabbage, beets, spinach, sweet corn, and leafy greens.

7. Saline Soil

Characteristics:

High salt content, found in arid regions.

Crops:

Barley, saltbush, some types of cotton and sugar beet (with proper management).
