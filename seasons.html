Seasons

Summer Season (March - June)

Suitable Crops:

Summer crops, also called warm-season crops, are vegetables that grow best in hot summertime weather. Popular summer crops to grow in your garden include corn, melons, tomatoes, zucchini, cucumbers, beans, sweet potatoes, peppers, and pumpkins.

Crops:

Corn (Maize), Soybeans, Tomatoes, Peppers, Cucumbers, and Melons.

Why:

These crops thrive in warm temperatures and have high sunlight requirements.

Not Suitable Crops:

Crops:

Lettuce, Spinach, Broccoli, Peas.

Losses:

These crops can bolt (go to seed) quickly in hot weather, reducing yield and quality.

Monsoon Season (June - September)

Suitable Crops:

Crops:

Rice, Maize, Sorghum, Cotton, Sugarcane.

Why:

These crops require a lot of water, and monsoon rains provide ample moisture.

Not Suitable Crops:

Crops:

Grapes, Onions, Garlic.

Losses:

Excessive rainfall can lead to fungal diseases and rot, damaging these crops.

Autumn Season (September - November)

Suitable Crops:

Crops:

Millet, Groundnuts (Peanuts), Chickpeas, Pulses.

Why:

Moderate temperatures and post-monsoon soil moisture are ideal for these crops.

Not Suitable Crops:

Crops:

Cauliflower, Cabbage (early autumn).

Losses:

Inconsistent temperatures and residual moisture can hinder growth and quality.

>

Winter Season (November - February)

Suitable Crops:

Crops:

Wheat, Barley, Mustard, Peas, Carrots, Spinach, Cabbage.

>Why:

These crops prefer cooler temperatures and can tolerate frost.

Not Suitable Crops:

Crops:

Rice, Okra, Eggplant (Brinjal).

Losses:

Cold weather can stunt growth and cause cold damage, leading to poor yields.

Spring Season (February - March)

Suitable Crops:

Crops:

Potatoes, Lentils, Coriander, Maize (for early summer harvest).

Why:

Mild temperatures and lengthening days support robust growth.

Not Suitable Crops:

Crops:

Winter-specific crops (those harvested late winter, such as late-planted wheat).

Losses:

Rising temperatures can negatively impact the growth and quality of winter crops.

  • By selecting the right crops for each season, farmers can maximize yields and minimize losses, ensuring a profitable and sustainable farming operation.
